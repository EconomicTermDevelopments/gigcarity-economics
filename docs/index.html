Gigcarity

Field: Labor Economics | JEL Codes: J81, J88, L86

Definition

The precarious economic conditions experienced by gig economy workers, characterized by income volatility, lack of employment benefits and protections, algorithmic management systems that shift risk to workers, and the absence of collective bargaining power typical of traditional employment relationships.

Etymology

Portmanteau of "gig" (short-term, flexible work arrangements mediated by digital platforms) + "precarity" (from Latin precarius, meaning "obtained by entreaty, uncertain"). The term elegantly fuses the employment model with its characteristic instability, creating a concise label for a widely-discussed but verbosely-described phenomenon.

Usage Example

The pandemic exposed how gigcarity renders platform workers particularly vulnerable to economic shocks, as food delivery couriers and rideshare drivers experienced dramatic income fluctuations without access to unemployment insurance or sick leave.
